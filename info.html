Mahatma Gandhi

The leader of the Indian National Congress, known for his philosophy of nonviolent resistance (Satyagraha).

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others."

Imam-ul Hind Maulana Abul Kalam Azad

An influential leader in India's freedom movement, Azad was a prominent scholar, journalist, and a senior leader of the Indian National Congress. He played a key role in the Khilafat Movement and worked towards Hindu-Muslim unity.

"The right to freedom of expression is my birthright, and I shall have it."

Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Architect of the Indian Constitution, advocate for Dalit rights.

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others."

Sarojini Naidu

Poet and political activist, first woman President of the Indian National Congress.

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others."

Jawaharlal Nehru

The first Prime Minister of independent India, a key leader in the Indian National Congress.

"The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others."

Subhas Chandra Bose

Leader of the Indian National Army, advocated for armed struggle against British rule.

"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

Maulana Mohammad Ali Jauhar

An important figure in the Khilafat Movement and Non-Cooperation Movement, Jauhar was a journalist, poet, and politician. He advocated for India's independence and the rights of Muslims.

"Die as a man, die as a martyr. That's the only way to die."

Begum Hazrat Mahal

She was a queen who played a significant role in the Indian Rebellion of 1857 (also known as the First War of Independence). She led the revolt against the British in Lucknow and fought for the restoration of the Mughal dynasty.

"I would prefer to live for only a day and die having played the part of a true Muslim woman."

Bhagat Singh

Part of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association, known for his revolutionary activities.

"Inquilab Zindabad!" ("Long live the revolution!")

Rani Lakshmibai

Leader in the Indian Rebellion of 1857, fought against British rule.

"Main apni Jhansi nahin doongi!" ("I will not surrender my Jhansi!)"

Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan (Bacha Khan):

A close associate of Mahatma Gandhi, Bacha Khan was a leader of the Khilafat Movement and the Indian National Congress. He was an advocate for nonviolent resistance and social reforms.

"I am a true believer in nonviolence, and I say that no peace or tranquility will descend upon the people of the world until nonviolence is practiced, because nonviolence is love and it stirs courage in people."

Umar Hayat Khan

A participant in the Kakori Conspiracy of 1925, he was a member of the Hindustan Republican Association and was involved in several actions against British rule

"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

Asaf Ali

An active participant in the Quit India Movement, Asaf Ali was a lawyer and a leader of the Indian National Congress. He played a significant role in the freedom struggle and was known for his legal acumen.

"Freedom is never given; it is won."

Maulana Shaukat Ali

A leader in the Khilafat Movement along with his brother Mohammad Ali Jauhar, Maulana Shaukat Ali played a significant role in mobilizing the masses against British rule.

""हम एक दिन वक़्त की गुलामी से बहाल होकर ख़ुद ख़ुद को मुक़ति देंगे।" ("We will one day free ourselves from the slavery of time and grant ourselves liberation.")

Dr. Zakir Husain

A prominent educationalist and political leader, Zakir Husain was the vice president and later the president of India. He actively participated in the freedom movement and worked for communal harmony.

"Education is not only a ladder of opportunity, but it is also an investment in our future."

Dadabhai Naoroji

First Indian to be elected to the British House of Commons, known as the "Grand Old Man of India."

""नहीं कोई भारत में उस महिला से बड़ा सत्याग्रही दुनिया में जिसने मातृभूमि के लिए अपने पति और बच्चों की प्यारी जिंदगी का परित्याग किया।" ("There is no greater practitioner of nonviolent resistance in India than that woman who sacrificed her beloved husband and children for the sake of the motherland.")"

Chandrashekhar Azad

Revolutionary who participated in various acts against British officials.

"Dushman ki goliyon ka hum samna karenge, Azad hi rahein hain, azad hi rahenge." ("We will face the bullets of the enemy; we are free and will remain free.")"

Hasrat Mohani

A poet, freedom fighter, and political leader, Hasrat Mohani was known for his patriotic poetry and active participation in the Indian National Congress and Khilafat Movement.
